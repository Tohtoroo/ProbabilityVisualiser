    • Bernoulli Distribution

    The Bernoulli distribution is a discrete probability distribution for a Bernoulli trial - a random experiment with only two outcomes (usually called a "Success" or a "Failure"). It is a special case of the binomial distribution where only a single trial is conducted, i.e. n = 1.

    A simple way to think of the Bernoulli distribution is through the flip of a biased coin. Suppose we have a coin which, when flipped, will land on its head with probability p. Then, the probability of it landing on its tail is 1 - p. In the Bernoulli trial, we can choose which side of the coin is a Success or a Failure. In this case, we deem heads to be Success. Therefore, the support of the probability mass function only contains 2 values, 0 for a failure, and 1 for success.
