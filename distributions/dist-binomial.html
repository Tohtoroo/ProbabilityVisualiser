    • Binomial Distribution

    The Binomial distribution is a discrete distribution which can be thought of as the probability of a Success or Failure outcome in an experiment that is conducted numerous times. It is the general case of the Bernoulli distribution where n trials are conducted.

    A Binomial distribution must meet the following criteria:

    • There are only two possible outcomes, Success or Failure.
    • The probability of Success, p, is the same for each trial.
    • The trials are independent of each other.
    • The number of trials, n, is fixed.
    • A random variable X = the number of Successes

      • Suppose we have coin which, when flipped, will land on its head with probability p and and tail with probability 1 - p. We flip this coin n times. In the Binomial, we keep track of n possible outcomes. The coin could land on its head 0 times, or 1 time, and so on, up till n times. Therefore, the support of the probability mass function is the set of integers from 0 to n, i.e. supp(f) = {0, 1, 2, ..., n}.
