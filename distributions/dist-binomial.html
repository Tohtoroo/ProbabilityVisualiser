    • Binomial Distribution

    The Binomial distribution is a general case of the Bernoulli distribution where n trials are conducted. Suppose we have a coin which, when flipped, will land on its head with probability p and and tail with probability 1 - p. We flip this coin n times. In the Binomial, we keep track of n possible outcomes. The coin could land on its head 0 times, or 1 time, and so on, up till n times. Therefore, the support of the probability mass function is the set of integers from 0 to n, i.e. supp(f) = {0, 1, 2, ..., n}. The graph below graphs the PMF of the Binomial Distribution.
