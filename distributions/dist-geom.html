    • Geometric Distribution

    The Geometric distribution is a discrete probability distribution that models the number of Bernoulli trials needed to get one Success, i.e the number of Failures before one Success. The probability for Success, p, across each trial must be constant. The distribution is the discrete analogue of the Exponential distribution, i.e it is the discrete version of the Exponential distribution. It is also the special case of the Negative Binomial distribution where the number of success is fixed at 1.

    For example, suppose we have a biased coin, which when flipped will have a probability p of landing heads and a probability 1-p of landing tails. Suppose we set that heads is the Success, and tails is the Failure. If we were to flip this coin until we get 1 heads, then the random variable X would be the number of flips needed to get 1 heads, i.e the number of Failures before getting 1 Success. Therefore, the distribution is showing what is the different probabilities for number of Failures needed before getting 1 Success.
