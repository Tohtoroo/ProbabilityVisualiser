The Gamma Distribution



In probability theory and statistics, the gamma distribution is a two-parameter family of continuous probability distributions. The exponential distribution, Erlang distribution, and chi-square distribution are special cases of the gamma distribution. We will use a shape parameter {\displaystyle \alpha =k}{\displaystyle \alpha =k} and an inverse scale parameter λ, also called a rate parameter, where both parameters are positive real numbers. The gamma distribution's importance is largely due to its relation to exponential and normal distributions.

The Gamma Function


The Gamma Function serves as one of the components of the Gamma Distribution. It is given by Γ(α)=∫∞0xα−1e−xdx,for α>0. The Gamma Function is a generalisation of the factorial function, such that Γ(n)=(n−1)!, for positive integers n.
